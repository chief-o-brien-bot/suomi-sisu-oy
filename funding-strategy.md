# Sisu Oy: Funding Strategy for AI-Powered Cooperative

## Executive Summary

Sisu Oy seeks €2-5M in seed funding to build an AI-powered cooperative that applies S-Group's proven model to digital infrastructure and collective bargaining. This document identifies Finnish and EU funding sources, pitch strategies for each, and lessons from successful Finnish cooperatives.

**Core Pitch:** What if 100K Finns collectively own digital infrastructure (VPN, storage, email) and AI agents negotiate their energy, insurance, and telecom contracts on their behalf? S-Group model + AI automation = massive collective bargaining leverage + profits flowing back to members.

---

## Why This is Fundable

### 1. Proven Cooperative Model
- **S-Group:** 2.4M Finnish members (44% of population), €13B revenue, market leader since 2005
- **OP Group:** 1.9M members, €14B revenue, successful banking cooperative
- **70% of Finns support cooperative model** (poll data)
- Cooperatives are culturally accepted and proven at massive scale in Finland

Sources: [S Group - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S_Group), [The Cooperative That Could - The American Prospect](https://prospect.org/world/2023-10-11-cooperative-that-could-s-group-finland/)

### 2. AI Makes it Scalable
- **2026 is the year AI moves from tool to infrastructure** for enterprises
- Autonomous contract negotiation proven with Fortune 500 (Pactum with Walmart, Maersk)
- **40% reduction in contract cycle times**, zero-touch agreements emerging
- What S-Group does manually (bulk purchasing), Sisu Oy automates with AI

Sources: [AI Contract Negotiation 2026 - Bind Legal](https://bindlegal.com/resources/guides/ai-contract-negotiation/), [AI Agents Are Taking Over Contract Negotiations - IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/ai-contracts)

### 3. Digital Sovereignty Imperative
- Growing concern about Finnish data leaving to US/EU cloud providers
- Government support for digital autonomy and European tech sovereignty
- EU investment in Digital Commons and infrastructure consortiums
- Data staying in Finland = competitive advantage + regulatory moat

Sources: [The Digital Europe Programme](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/activities/digital-programme)

### 4. Economic Innovation for AI Era
- New model for distributing AI-generated value
- Collective bargaining leverage: 100K members = transformative negotiating power
- AI-owned businesses where profits flow to members, not VCs
- Addresses AI wealth concentration proactively

### 5. VPN-First De-Risks Launch
- Proof of concept with lowest-risk service (VPN)
- Technically simple (WireGuard, battle-tested)
- Low trust requirement compared to email/storage
- Can validate cooperative model and dividend distribution before scaling to collective bargaining

## Lessons from S-Group and OP Group Early Funding

### How They Started (1902-1904)

**Capital Source:** Member contributions from local farmers and communities
- Not traditional VC or foundation funding
- Built on small investments from thousands of members
- Regional cooperatives formed first, then federated into national organizations

**Key Insight for Sisu Oy:**
The early cooperatives were **member-funded, not foundation-funded**. They succeeded because:
1. Members had immediate incentive (collective purchasing power)
2. Small contributions from many (not large investment from few)
3. Regional autonomy (local cooperatives controlled by local members)
4. Gradual federation (central organization came after regional success)

Source: [History of the cooperative movement in Finland - Pellervo](https://pellervo.fi/en/english/history-of-the-cooperative-movement-in-finland/)

### Application to Sisu Oy

**Hybrid Funding Strategy:**
1. **Foundation funding (€2-5M):** Build MVP, validate model, reach 1K-10K members
2. **Member pre-investment:** Early members pay membership fee (€20-50) that functions as seed capital
3. **Revenue funding:** VPN subscriptions generate cash flow to fund expansion
4. **Growth capital:** Once model is proven, seek larger institutional investment or EU programs

**Member Pre-Investment Model:**
- €20 refundable membership fee × 1,000 early members = €20K seed capital
- €20 refundable membership fee × 10,000 members = €200K additional capital
- At 100K members = €2M in member capital (refundable but available for operations)

**This mirrors S-Group/OP Group model:** Members fund the cooperative they own and benefit from.

## Crowdfunding + Membership Hybrid

### Concept
Combine traditional foundation funding with member pre-investment through a regulated crowdfunding campaign.

**Finnish Crowdfunding Regulations:**
- Finland allows equity crowdfunding and cooperative share offerings
- Could structure as "buy membership + VPN subscription" bundle
- Members get immediate service + ownership stake + future dividends

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Validates demand (if 1K Finns pre-pay, model has product-market fit)
- ✅ Builds community before launch
- ✅ Generates capital without diluting cooperative ownership
- ✅ Marketing: every member is an evangelist
- ✅ Aligns with cooperative ethos (member-funded, member-owned)

**Example Structure:**
- **Founding Member Package:** €100 (€20 membership + €80 = 1 year VPN prepaid)
- Target: 1,000 founding members = €100K
- If combined with €1M Sitra grant = €1.1M total runway

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Refundable membership fee (members can exit and get €20 back)
- VPN service delivered within 6 months (tangible product, not vaporware)
- Transparent roadmap: exactly what milestones unlock at what member counts
