# Sisu Oy: MVP Strategy - Founder's Circle + VPN Hybrid Model

**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Purpose:** Define which service(s) to launch first and how to structure the MVP to prove the Sisu Oy cooperative model

---

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Launch Founder's Circle Membership + VPN simultaneously (not VPN-only or energy-only).**

This hybrid model addresses the fatal flaws identified in the steel-man critique while preserving the strengths of the original strategy:

**What Makes This Work:**
1. **VPN** = immediate value delivery + trust building + technical proof-of-concept
2. **Founder's Circle** = pre-commitment to collective bargaining + upfront capital + network effects from day one
3. **Milestone Roadmap** = transparent path to energy savings + viral recruitment incentive
4. **Two revenue streams** = €50K upfront (founding memberships) + €30K recurring (VPN subscriptions) in Year 1

**Key Insight from Critique:**
> "VPN-first creates cold start problem (70% failure probability). Need network effects from day one to reach 10K members for collective bargaining."

**How Hybrid Model Solves This:**
- Founder's Circle creates network effects immediately (progress toward 1,000-member milestone)
- VPN builds trust with low-stakes service while members wait for collective bargaining
- Pre-commitment model ensures members stick around for Phase 3 (not just churn after trying VPN)

**Financial Reality:**
- Year 1: -€132K loss (vs -€292K in original VPN-only plan)
- Year 4: +€260K profit (break-even Month 48-50)
- Total capital to profitability: €401K (well within €2M seed round)

## The Four Service Options (From Original Analysis)

### Option 1: VPN
**Original Assessment:** WINNER (lowest risk, fastest to market, highest margins)

**Post-Critique Reality Check:**
- ✅ Lowest technical risk
- ✅ Fast development (1-3 months)
- ✅ High margins (14% gross, but good at scale)
- ❌ **FATAL FLAW: Zero network effects** (1 user = 1,000 users in value)
- ❌ **Commoditized market** (NordVPN €100M marketing spend)
- ❌ **Wrong customer segment** (privacy nerds ≠ collective bargaining users)
- ❌ **18-month runway to 10K members = impossible** (critique showed 70% failure probability)

**Revised Assessment:** VPN is necessary but NOT sufficient as standalone MVP.

### Option 2: Cloud Storage
**Original Assessment:** Second place (high trust requirement, good stickiness)

**Post-Critique Reality:**
- Medium technical risk
- 3-6 months development
- Storage costs scale with users (not pure margin like VPN)
- **Better than VPN for trust building** (more valuable data = higher stakes = trust proof)
- But: Still no network effects

**Revised Assessment:** Good Phase 2 service after VPN trust is established.

### Option 3: Password Manager
**Original Assessment:** Third place (perfect trust anchor, but highest security risk)

**Post-Critique Reality:**
- HIGH security risk (single breach = death of company)
- 4-6 months development + ongoing security audits
- Most sticky service (nightmare to migrate)
- **Best trust anchor, but too risky for MVP**

**Revised Assessment:** Phase 3 service after security competence proven.

### Option 4: Email Service
**Original Assessment:** Fourth place (complex, high uptime requirement)

**Post-Critique Reality:**
- Most complex service (SMTP, spam filtering, deliverability)
- 6-12 months development
- Very sticky (changing email = painful)
- **Too complex for MVP**

**Revised Assessment:** Phase 4+ service, if ever.

## The Missing Option: Energy Collective Bargaining

**Not analyzed in original card, but identified as optimal in solutions document.**

### Why Energy Wasn't in Original Analysis

The original card evaluated *digital services* only (VPN, storage, password manager, email). Energy collective bargaining wasn't considered because it's not a "service you build" — it's a negotiation capability.

### Why Critique Recommends Energy-Only Launch

**Critique's energy-only recommendation:**
1. Network effects from day one (more members = better pricing)
2. Immediate measurable value (€100-200/year savings vs €60/year VPN)
3. Natural viral growth (people tell friends about €200 savings, not VPN)
4. No trust deficit (not storing data, just negotiating contracts)
5. Proven model (S-Group Energia works exactly this way)

**Timeline comparison:**
- VPN-first to 10K members: 18-36 months (maybe)
- Energy-first to 10K members: 12-18 months (word-of-mouth from savings)

### Why Energy-Only Has Problems Too

**Challenges with energy-only launch:**
1. **High initial trust requirement** (members must share energy usage data, contracts)
2. **No proof of technical competence** (how do members know you won't mess up their energy contracts?)
3. **Complex onboarding** (energy contract transfers are harder than VPN signup)
4. **Regulatory risk** (energy negotiation may require licenses before launch)

**The trust problem:**
- VPN breach = annoying
- Energy contract breach = members have no electricity
- **Energy-only asks for high trust before proving competence**

## The Solution: Hybrid Model (Founder's Circle + VPN)

**Launch two products simultaneously:**

### Product 1: VPN (Immediate Value)
- €5/month subscription
- Low-stakes trust builder
- Proves technical competence
- **Delivers value while members wait for collective bargaining**

### Product 2: Founder's Circle Membership (Future Value)
- €100/year founding membership
- Pre-commits to collective bargaining when unlocked
- Grants governance rights + 2× dividend share for 5 years
- **Creates network effects from day one**

### Transparent Milestone Roadmap

Members see exactly when collective bargaining unlocks:

**1,000 members:** Energy collective bargaining activated (€100-200/year savings)
**5,000 members:** Insurance collective bargaining activated (€150-300/year savings)
**10,000 members:** Telecom collective bargaining activated (€100-200/year savings)
**25,000 members:** Full AI-powered autonomous negotiation

### Member Value Proposition

**VPN-Only Member:**
- Pay: €5/month (€60/year)
- Get: VPN access only
- Collective bargaining: Unlocks at 10,000 total members
- Net value Year 1: -€60 (cost)

**Founding Member:**
- Pay: €100/year
- Get: VPN access + collective bargaining (unlocks at 1,000 members) + 2× dividends + governance rights
- Net value Year 1: -€100 (cost)
- Net value Year 2 (after energy unlocks): +€100-200 (savings exceed founding fee)
- **Payback period: 12-18 months**

**Why this works:**
- Founding membership pays for itself once collective bargaining unlocks
- VPN-only members see what they're missing (conversion funnel)
- Early adopters rewarded (2× dividend bonus)
- **Clear value proposition for both tiers**

## Why Hybrid Model > VPN-Only or Energy-Only

### Hybrid vs VPN-Only

**VPN-only problems:**
- Zero network effects
- 18-month runway to 10K members (impossible)
- Wrong customer segment (privacy nerds don't care about collective bargaining)
- 70% failure probability (per critique)

**How hybrid solves this:**
- Founder's Circle creates network effects immediately (progress bar to 1,000)
- Members pre-commit to collective bargaining (not just trying VPN)
- Two customer segments: privacy-focused (VPN-only) + value-focused (founding members)
- **40-60% success probability** (per solutions document)

### Hybrid vs Energy-Only

**Energy-only problems:**
- High initial trust requirement (members share energy data before you prove competence)
- No incremental trust building (all-or-nothing)
- Regulatory risk (may need licenses before launch)

**How hybrid solves this:**
- VPN builds trust incrementally (low-stakes first, high-stakes later)
- Founding members pre-commit but don't share energy data until 1,000 members
- Time to get regulatory opinions (energy law, insurance intermediary licensing)
- **Phased trust building**

### Comparison Table

| Approach | Network Effects | Trust Requirement | Time to Value | Failure Probability |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| VPN-Only | None | Low | Immediate (VPN) | 70% (cold start) |
| Energy-Only | Strong | High | 6 months (energy savings) | 40% (trust deficit) |
| **Hybrid (Founder's Circle + VPN)** | **Strong** | **Low → High** | **Immediate (VPN) + 12 months (energy)** | **30-40%** |

**Hybrid model is best of both worlds:**
- Network effects like energy-only (milestone progress)
- Low initial trust like VPN-only (prove competence first)
- Immediate value delivery (VPN) + future value promise (energy)

## MVP Feature Specification

### Phase 1A: Founder's Circle Pre-Launch (Months 1-3)

**Goal:** 500 founding members before VPN launch

**What to build:**
- Landing page with explainer (sisu.fi)
- Founder's Circle signup form
- Payment processing (Stripe, Finnish bank transfer)
- Member dashboard (basic)
  - Membership status
  - Progress bar: X/1,000 members (energy unlock)
  - Share link for referrals
  - Founding member badge

**What NOT to build:**
- No VPN yet
- No collective bargaining yet
- Just: Signup → payment → membership status

**Marketing:**
- S-Group/OP partnership discussions (co-marketing)
- Tech communities (Helsinki developers, Aalto students)
- Trade unions (bulk memberships)
- **Target: 500 founding members × €100 = €50K upfront capital**

**Success criteria:**
- 300+ founding members by Month 3 (go/no-go decision)
- If under 300, consider pivot or refund

### Phase 1B: VPN MVP Launch (Months 4-6)

**Goal:** Deliver VPN to founding members, open to VPN-only members

**What to build:**
- WireGuard-based VPN server (Hetzner Finland)
- VPN client apps:
  - macOS (WireGuard GUI + custom branding)
  - Windows (WireGuard GUI + custom branding)
  - Linux (command-line)
  - iOS (WireGuard app config export)
  - Android (WireGuard app config export)
- Member portal:
  - VPN config download
  - Connection status
  - Usage stats
  - Support chat
- Open-source everything (GitHub: github.com/sisu-oy)

**What NOT to build:**
- No custom VPN protocol (use WireGuard)
- No fancy features (kill switch, split tunneling) in MVP
- No mobile native apps (use official WireGuard app)
- **MVP = functional VPN only**

**Infrastructure:**
- 2-3 servers in Finland (Helsinki, Tampere)
- 1 Gbps bandwidth
- Cost: €100-150/month for 1,000 users

**Marketing:**
- Open VPN-only tier (€5/month)
- Founding members get VPN free as part of membership
- Continue recruiting founding members (target: 1,000 total)

**Success criteria:**
- VPN works reliably (>99% uptime)
- <1% churn in first 3 months (proof of quality)
- 800+ total members by Month 6

### Phase 1C: Collective Bargaining Unlock (Months 7-12)

**Goal:** Hit 1,000 members, activate energy collective bargaining

**What to build:**
- Energy enrollment form (members opt-in to collective bargaining)
- Contract management dashboard (members see their energy contract status)
- Energy procurement specialist hired (€60K/year)
- Legal opinions completed:
  - Energy law (€30K)
  - Competition law submission to FCCA (€20K)

**How energy bargaining works:**
- 1,000 members opt-in to energy collective bargaining
- Procurement specialist collects: current provider, usage, preferences (price vs green)
- Specialist negotiates with 3-5 Finnish energy providers (Fortum, Helen, OP Energia, Vattenfall)
- Procurement specialist presents best deals to members
- Members choose: accept group rate or stay with current provider
- **Savings target: 10-15% = €100-200/year per member**

**What NOT to build:**
- No automatic switching (members manually sign new contracts)
- No AI negotiation (human specialist only)
- **Manual process, prove concept first**

**Success criteria:**
- 1,000 members enrolled
- Energy collective bargaining delivers 10-15% savings
- 90%+ member satisfaction with energy deal
- **Founding members' €100 fee pays for itself in Year 1**

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Target Customer Segments (Prioritized)

**Segment 1: S-Group Loyalists (Primary Target)**
- **Size:** 2.4M Finnish households
- **Psychographic:** Already value cooperative ownership (choose S-Market over Lidl)
- **Value prop:** "You shop at S-Market because you own it. Why don't you use a VPN you own?"
- **CAC:** €5-10 (S-Group co-marketing)
- **Why this works:** Values alignment (cooperative > corporate)

**Segment 2: OP Group Members**
- **Size:** 1.9M Finnish households
- **Psychographic:** Cooperative bank members, trust cooperatives
- **Value prop:** "OP members get 10% founding member discount"
- **CAC:** €5-10 (OP partnership)
- **Why this works:** Existing cooperative relationship

**Segment 3: Trade Union Members**
- **Size:** 1.9M Finnish workers (SAK, STTK, Akava)
- **Psychographic:** Understand collective power
- **Value prop:** "Collective bargaining for your digital services, not just your wages"
- **CAC:** €2-5 (union bulk purchases)
- **Why this works:** Unions negotiate bulk rates for members (same model)

**Segment 4: Tech Workers & Students**
- **Size:** 100K+ (Helsinki tech scene, universities)
- **Psychographic:** Early adopters, privacy-conscious, open-source supporters
- **Value prop:** "Open-source VPN + cooperative ownership = can't enshittify"
- **CAC:** €10-15 (developer communities, university partnerships)
- **Why this works:** Values transparency + data sovereignty

**DO NOT TARGET:** General "privacy nerds" (too competitive, wrong segment)

### Marketing Channels (Prioritized)

**Channel 1: S-Group Partnership (If Secured)**
- Approach: "We build the tech, you provide members and brand"
- Co-marketing: S-Group newsletter, in-store signage, S-mobiili app integration
- **Potential reach: 2.4M customers**
- **If successful: 10K members in 6 months** (game-changer)

**Channel 2: OP Group Partnership (Parallel Track)**
- Similar structure to S-Group
- OP has digital banking app (excellent distribution)
- **Potential reach: 1.9M customers**

**Channel 3: Trade Unions (Bulk Deals)**
- Pitch union chapters: "Offer Sisu founding membership as member benefit"
- Union pays €50/member, members get €100 value (union subsidizes)
- **Target: 5-10 union chapters × 100 members each = 500-1,000 members**

**Channel 4: Content Marketing (Open Source Community)**
- GitHub repo with full transparency
- Technical blog posts (how we built the VPN, security audits)
- r/Finland, Finnish tech Slack/Discord communities
- **CAC: €0-5 (organic growth)**

**Channel 5: Referral Program**
- Founding members get 1 month free VPN for each successful referral
- Progress bar creates FOMO ("help us reach 1,000 to unlock energy")
- **Viral coefficient target: 0.3-0.5** (each member brings 0.3-0.5 more members)

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ Facebook/Google ads (€50-80 CAC, competing with NordVPN)
- ❌ Influencer sponsorships (expensive, wrong audience)
- ❌ Mass market advertising (waste of money)

### Pricing Strategy

**VPN-Only Tier:**
- €5/month or €50/year (annual discount)
- Matches Mullvad, IVPN (credible mid-tier pricing)
- No freemium (freemium users don't convert to founding members)

**Founding Member Tier:**
- €100/year
- Includes: VPN + collective bargaining (when unlocked) + 2× dividends + governance
- **Positioning:** "€100 pays for itself in Year 2 through energy savings"

**Student Discount:**
- €3/month VPN or €60/year founding membership (40% off)
- Must verify with university email
- **Hook students early, convert to full price after graduation**

**Trade Union Bulk:**
- €50-80/member (union negotiated rate)
- Unions buy founding memberships for members as benefit
- **B2B sales to unions, not individual sales**

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Targets

**Year 1:**
- Target CAC: €10-15 (via partnerships)
- Acceptable CAC: €20-30 (if organic channels fail)
- **Never exceed €50 CAC** (unprofitable)

**Year 2-3:**

**Year 4+:**
- Target CAC: €10-20 (majority word-of-mouth, partnerships mature)

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Can't Reach 500 Founding Members

**Probability:** Medium (20-30%)
**Impact:** High (no upfront capital, weak signal for collective bargaining)

**Mitigations:**
- Lower founding membership to €50/year (accessible to more people)
- Extend pre-launch period to 6 months (more time to recruit)
- Offer refund guarantee ("if we don't hit 1,000 members by Month 18, full refund")
- **Go/no-go at 300 members:** If under 300 by Month 3, reassess

### Risk 2: VPN Quality Issues

**Probability:** Low (10-15%)
**Impact:** Very High (trust destroyed, members churn)

**Mitigations:**
- Use proven tech (WireGuard, not custom VPN protocol)
- Beta test with 50 members before full launch
- 24/7 uptime monitoring (PagerDuty, UptimeRobot)
- Over-provision bandwidth (2× expected usage)
- **Quality > features in MVP**

### Risk 3: Energy Companies Refuse to Negotiate

**Probability:** Low (15-20%)
**Impact:** High (collective bargaining doesn't unlock, founding members feel betrayed)

**Mitigations:**
- Begin energy negotiations in Month 4 (before 1,000 members)
- Approach 5-10 providers (only need 1-2 to say yes)
- If all refuse, partner with S-Group Energia or OP Energia (co-op to co-op)
- **Fallback:** Energy advisory model (recommend best deals, don't negotiate)

### Risk 4: VPN Users Don't Care About Collective Bargaining

**Probability:** Medium (25-35%)
**Impact:** Medium (separate customer segments, but both viable)

**Mitigations:**
- Founding member tier explicitly for collective bargaining users
- VPN-only tier explicitly for privacy users
- **Hybrid model allows both segments to coexist**
- If bridge fails, accept two separate businesses

### Risk 5: S-Group/OP Partnership Fails

**Probability:** High (50-60%)
**Impact:** Medium (higher CAC, slower growth, but still viable)

**Mitigations:**
- Don't depend on partnerships (bootstrap independently)
- Partnerships are accelerator, not requirement
- Trade unions as alternative distribution channel
- **Financial model assumes no partnership** (partnerships are upside)
