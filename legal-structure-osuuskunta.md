# Sisu Oy: Osuuskunta Legal Structure Deep Dive

**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Purpose:** Comprehensive legal analysis of Finnish osuuskunta (cooperative) structure for Sisu Oy, including governance, membership mechanics, dividend distribution, and anti-enshittification protections

---

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation:** Incorporate Sisu Oy as osuuskunta (Finnish cooperative) under Osuuskuntalaki 421/2013.

**Why Osuuskunta:**
1. ✅ Member-ownership built into legal structure (not optional bylaws)
2. ✅ Proven at massive scale (S-Group 2.5M members, OP Group 2M members)
3. ✅ Cultural familiarity (Finns understand cooperative model)
4. ✅ Avoids securities regulation (cooperative shares ≠ securities)
5. ✅ Anti-enshittification protections are legally enforceable
6. ✅ One member, one vote principle enshrined in law (with flexibility)

**Key Decisions for Sisu Oy:**
- **Membership Model:** Hybrid (automatic membership upon subscription + optional founding membership tier)
- **Governance:** Representative democracy (elected board + mandatory member vote on major changes)
- **Voting:** Modified one member, one vote (base 1 vote + bonus votes for tenure/usage, capped at 5× multiplier)
- **Dividends:** Usage-weighted (80% to members proportional to subscription value, 20% to reserves)
- **Anti-Enshittification:** Supermajority (75%) member approval required for acquisition, data policy changes, or mission changes

**Formation Timeline:**
- Week 1: Draft cooperative bylaws
- Week 2: Recruit minimum 3 founding members (board members)
- Week 3: Register with PRH (Finnish Patent and Registration Office)
- Week 4: Incorporated, can begin operations
- **Cost:** €175 registration fee + €1K-2K legal consultation

## Part 1: Legal Structure Basics

### 1.1 Osuuskunta vs Oy (Limited Company)

| Feature | Osuuskunta (Cooperative) | Oy (Limited Company) |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Ownership** | Member-owned (members are customers) | Shareholder-owned (shareholders ≠ customers) |
| **Purpose** | Support members' economy/business | Maximize shareholder profit |
| **Capital** | Variable (members join/leave) | Fixed (shares traded) |
| **Liability** | Members NOT liable for co-op debts | Shareholders NOT liable (limited) |
| **Voting** | One member, one vote (default) | One share, one vote (proportional) |
| **Dividends** | Based on usage (ylijäämänpalautus) | Based on share ownership |
| **Governance** | Democratic (member assembly) | Shareholder-driven |
| **Acquisition** | Difficult (members must approve) | Easy (buy majority shares) |
| **Securities Regulation** | NO (co-op shares not securities) | YES (shares are securities) |
| **Cultural Fit** | High (S-Group/OP model familiar) | Low (perceived as "corporate") |

**Verdict:** Osuuskunta is vastly superior for Sisu Oy's "anti-enshittification" positioning. Oy could achieve *some* protections through special bylaws, but osuuskunta makes member ownership the default legal structure.

### 1.2 Formation Requirements

**Minimum Requirements (Per Osuuskuntalaki 421/2013):**

**Founders:**
- Minimum 3 founding members required
- At least one board member must be permanently domiciled in EEA (EU + Norway/Iceland/Liechtenstein)
- No minimum capital requirement (unlike Oy which requires €2,500)

**Registration Process:**
1. **Draft cooperative bylaws** (rules must include compulsory provisions per Chapter 3, Section 3)
2. **Hold founding meeting** (3+ founders adopt bylaws, elect first board)
3. **Register with PRH** (Finnish Patent and Registration Office)
4. **Cost:** €175 registration fee
5. **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks from submission to incorporation

**Name Requirements:**
- Must contain "osuuskunta" OR "osuus" OR abbreviation "osk"
- Examples: "Sisu Osuuskunta," "Osuuskunta Sisu," "Sisu Osk"
- English translation: "Sisu Cooperative" (for international branding)

**Digital Registration (2026 Update):**
- Must use online service at ytj.fi (no paper forms accepted after Jan 1, 2026)
- All documents submitted electronically

**Board Composition:**
- At least one actual board member + one deputy member must be EEA-domiciled
- Managing Director (if appointed) must be EEA-domiciled or have PRH permit
- **Implication for Sisu Oy:** Finnish citizens on board required, but members can be global

### 1.3 Governance Requirements

**Mandatory Governing Bodies (Per Osuuskuntalaki):**

**1. General Assembly (Osuuskunnan kokous)**
- Highest decision-making body
- All members have right to participate and vote
- Must be held at least once per year (Annual General Meeting)
- Can be replaced by "Members' Council" if cooperative is large (S-Group uses this model)

**2. Board of Directors (Hallitus)**
- Responsible for operations and strategy
- Elected by General Assembly (or Members' Council)
- Minimum 1 member, but typically 3-7 for credibility
- Term length defined in bylaws (typically 1-3 years)

**3. Managing Director (Optional, Toimitusjohtaja)**
- Appointed by Board
- Day-to-day operations
- **Not mandatory** for small cooperatives (board can handle operations)

**4. Supervisory Board (Optional, Hallintoneuvosto)**
- Oversees board of directors
- Used by large cooperatives (S-Group has this)
- **Not needed for Sisu Oy initially**

### 1.4 Member Rights and Obligations

**Member Rights (Legally Guaranteed):**
1. **Voting Rights:** One member, one vote (default, can be modified in bylaws with 20× cap)
2. **Dividend Rights:** Share in ylijäämänpalautus (surplus distribution) if bylaws allow
3. **Information Rights:** Access to financial statements, board reports
4. **Governance Rights:** Attend General Assembly, propose motions, run for board
5. **Exit Rights:** Resign membership (bylaws define notice period, typically 3-12 months)

**Member Obligations:**
1. **Membership Fee:** Pay initial membership fee (amount defined in bylaws)
2. **Share Purchase (Optional):** Bylaws can require purchasing cooperative shares
3. **Service Usage:** Cooperatives typically require members use the services (Sisu Oy: subscribe to VPN/storage/etc.)
4. **Bylaw Compliance:** Follow cooperative rules

**Member Liability:**
- **NO personal liability** for cooperative's debts (per Osuuskuntalaki)
- Members only risk their membership fee + share capital (if any)
- This is identical to Oy (limited liability)

## Part 2: Membership Mechanics for Sisu Oy

### 2.1 Can Membership Be Automatic Upon Subscription?

**Legal Answer:** Yes, but with important caveats.

**How Automatic Membership Works:**

**Option A: Automatic Membership (S-Group Model)**
- User signs up for VPN (€5/month subscription)
- Bylaws state: "Subscription automatically grants membership"
- User becomes member upon first payment
- **Pros:** Seamless onboarding, all customers are owners
- **Cons:** High churn = many members joining/leaving (administrative burden)

**Option B: Separate Membership Tier (OP Model)**
- VPN-only users are customers (NOT members)
- Founding membership (€100/year) grants ownership + governance rights
- **Pros:** Members are committed (paid upfront), lower admin burden
- **Cons:** Creates two-tier system (some customers aren't owners)

**Option C: Hybrid (Recommended for Sisu Oy)**
- **Automatic membership for all subscribers** (inclusive, aligns with mission)
- **Founding membership tier** (€100/year) grants extra benefits:
  - 2× voting power (within legal 20× cap)
  - 2× dividend share
  - Early access to new services
  - Founding member badge
- **Rationale:** All customers are owners (mission alignment), but founding members have higher stake (aligns with higher commitment)

**Legal Requirements for Automatic Membership:**

1. **Bylaws must explicitly state automatic membership:**
   > "Any natural or legal person who subscribes to Sisu Osuuskunta services automatically becomes a member upon first payment."

2. **Membership fee = subscription fee (for automatic members):**
   - VPN-only subscriber: €5/month subscription IS the membership fee
   - Founding member: €100/year founding fee IS the membership fee (includes all services)

3. **Resignation process must be defined:**
   - User cancels subscription → automatic resignation from membership
   - Membership fee (share capital, if any) refunded within X days
   - Bylaws define notice period (recommend: 0 days for automatic members, instant cancellation)

4. **Members must be informed of their rights:**
   - Signup flow includes: "By subscribing, you become a member of Sisu Osuuskunta with voting rights and dividend eligibility."
   - Checkbox: "I understand I am becoming an owner-member of the cooperative."

**Tax Implications of Automatic Membership:**
- Membership fee refunds upon resignation are treated as taxable capital gains (per Finnish tax authority)
- For Sisu Oy: If user cancels VPN (€5/month), there's no "refund" (they just stop paying), so no tax event
- **But:** If user paid €100 founding membership and resigns, that refund IS taxable capital gain

**Precedent:**
- S-Group uses this model: Pay €100 membership fee → become member → get S-Etukortti → shop at stores
- OP Group uses separate model: Become OP customer (not automatic member), then optionally become owner-customer
- Sisu Oy can choose either, but hybrid (automatic membership + optional founding tier) is recommended

### 2.2 Can Non-Finns Be Members? (EU Expansion)

**Legal Answer:** Yes, with caveats.

**General Members:**
- **No restriction on nationality** for general members
- Any natural or legal person can be a member (per Osuuskuntalaki)
- **Implication:** Swedish, Estonian, German, etc. residents can join Sisu Oy as members

**Board Members / Managing Director:**
- **At least one board member** must be permanently domiciled in EEA (EU + Norway/Iceland/Liechtenstein)
- Managing Director must be EEA-domiciled OR have PRH permit
- **Implication:** Finnish board member required, but other board members can be non-Finnish (as long as one is EEA)

**Cross-Border Services:**
- Finnish osuuskunta can provide services across EU (single market)
- Members in Sweden, Estonia, Germany can use Sisu VPN under same cooperative
- **No need for separate national cooperatives** (unlike, e.g., S-Group which has regional structure within Finland)

**Tax Implications:**
- Members outside Finland: Dividend taxation depends on their residence (tax treaties apply)
- Sisu Oy must comply with tax reporting in members' countries (complex, but manageable with accounting software)

**Recommended Structure for EU Expansion:**
- **Phase 1 (Finland only):** Launch with Finnish members, Finnish board
- **Phase 2 (Nordic):** Open to Swedish, Norwegian, Danish members (same cooperative, same bylaws)
- **Phase 3 (EU-wide):** Open to all EU residents
- **No separate cooperatives needed** (one Sisu Osuuskunta serves all EU members)

**Precedent:**
- Many Finnish cooperatives are Finland-only (S-Group, OP Group)
- But some European cooperatives operate cross-border (e.g., FENNIA Insurance has Nordic presence)
- Sisu Oy's digital-first model makes EU-wide membership easier than physical retail

## Part 3: Shares and Capital

### 3.1 Membership Fees vs Share Capital

**Finnish cooperatives have TWO types of financial contributions:**

**1. Membership Fee (Jäsenmaksu)**
- **Purpose:** Entry fee to join the cooperative
- **Amount:** Defined in bylaws (typically €10-€500)
- **Refundable:** Depends on bylaws (can be fully/partially/non-refundable)
- **S-Group example:** €100 membership fee (fully refundable upon resignation)

**2. Share Capital (Osuuspääoma)**
- **Purpose:** Cooperative's equity capital
- **Structure:** Members buy shares (e.g., €10 per share, minimum 1 share)
- **Refundable:** Yes, upon resignation (at original value, not market value)
- **OP Group example:** Members buy shares, receive interest on share capital

**Recommendation for Sisu Oy:**

**Option A: Subscription-as-Membership-Fee (No Separate Share Capital)**
- VPN-only members: €5/month subscription = membership fee (no separate shares)
- Founding members: €100/year founding fee = membership fee (no separate shares)
- **Pros:** Simplest structure, no capital refund complexity
- **Cons:** Cooperative has no permanent capital base

**Option B: Membership Fee + Share Purchase**
- All members: Pay €20 membership fee + buy 1 share (€10) = €30 total
- VPN subscription (€5/month) is separate from membership/shares
- Upon resignation: €30 refunded (membership fee + share capital)
- **Pros:** Cooperative builds permanent capital base
- **Cons:** More complex, refund liability grows with members

**Recommended: Option A (Subscription-as-Membership-Fee)**
- **Rationale:** Sisu Oy is subscription business, not capital-intensive
- Members pay ongoing subscriptions (€5/month or €100/year), this IS the membership fee
- No need for separate share capital (cooperative funded by subscriptions, not member capital)
- Upon resignation: No refund (user just stops paying subscription)
- **Exception:** Founding members who paid €100 upfront may expect refund if they resign within first year

**Share Value Changes:**
- Cooperative shares are NOT traded (unlike Oy shares)
- Share value = nominal value (doesn't change)
- **No capital gains for members** (shares don't appreciate)
- **But:** Dividends provide financial return

## Part 4: Dividend Distribution (Ylijäämänpalautus)

### 4.1 Legal Framework for Dividends

**Finnish Cooperative Dividends = Ylijäämänpalautus (Surplus Distribution)**

**How It Works:**
1. Cooperative generates surplus (revenue - costs)
2. Board proposes dividend distribution
3. General Assembly approves distribution
4. Surplus distributed to members

**Legal Requirements (Per Osuuskuntalaki):**
- Dividends CAN be distributed (not mandatory)
- Distribution formula defined in bylaws
- **Members' surplus distribution based on usage** (not share ownership)
- **This is the key difference from Oy dividends** (which are per-share)

**Tax Treatment:**
- **Ylijäämänpalautus is taxable income** for members (personal income tax)
- **Cooperative can deduct it** as expense (reduces corporate tax)
- **Net effect:** Surplus taxed at member level (personal tax rates), not corporate level

**Comparison to Oy Dividends:**
- Oy dividends = per share (1,000 shares = 10× dividends of 100 shares)
- Cooperative dividends = per usage (spend €1,000 at S-Group = 10× dividends of €100 spent)

**Sources:**
- [Finnish tax authority on cooperative surplus](https://www.vero.fi/en/About-us/contact-us/forms/filling-instructions/annual-information-return-on-capital-refunds-taxable-as-capital-gains-instructions-2025/)
- [Paying dividends in Finland](https://www.vero.fi/en/businesses-and-corporations/business-operations/financial-sector/paying-dividends/)

### 4.2 Dividend Distribution Formula for Sisu Oy

**Recommended: Usage-Weighted Dividends**

**Formula:**
- **80% of surplus** distributed to members
- **20% of surplus** retained for reserves/operating capital
- Members' share proportional to subscription value (usage-based)

**Example Calculation:**

**Year 1 Scenario:**
- Sisu Oy surplus: €500K
- 10,000 members:
  - 5,000 VPN-only members (€5/month = €60/year each)
  - 5,000 founding members (€100/year each)

**Total subscription value:**
- VPN-only: 5,000 × €60 = €300K
- Founding: 5,000 × €100 = €500K
- **Total: €800K**

**Dividend pool:** €500K × 80% = €400K

**Individual dividends:**
- VPN-only member: (€60 / €800K) × €400K = €30 dividend (50% of subscription cost)
- Founding member: (€100 / €800K) × €400K = €50 dividend (50% of subscription cost)

**But wait:** Founding members should get 2× dividend (per solutions document)

**Adjusted formula with founding member bonus:**
- Founding members get 2× weight in dividend calculation
- VPN-only: Weight = 1.0
- Founding: Weight = 2.0

**Weighted subscription value:**
- VPN-only: 5,000 × €60 × 1.0 = €300K
- Founding: 5,000 × €100 × 2.0 = €1M
- **Total weighted: €1.3M**

**Adjusted dividends:**
- VPN-only member: (€60 × 1.0 / €1.3M) × €400K = €18 dividend
- Founding member: (€100 × 2.0 / €1.3M) × €400K = €61 dividend

**Result:**
- VPN-only: Pay €60, get €18 dividend, net €42 cost
- Founding: Pay €100, get €61 dividend, net €39 cost (better value!)

**Legal Compliance:**
- This is legal under Osuuskuntalaki (bylaws can define dividend formula)
- Must be clearly stated in bylaws: "Founding members receive 2× dividend weight"
- **Precedent:** S-Group uses tiered bonus system (spend more = higher % bonus)

### 4.3 Dividend Distribution: Equal vs Usage-Based vs Tenure-Based

**Three Models for Cooperative Dividends:**

**Model A: Equal Per Member (Pure Egalitarian)**
- Every member gets equal dividend (regardless of usage)
- Example: €500K surplus, 10,000 members = €50 per member
- **Pros:** Simplest, most "democratic"
- **Cons:** Unfair (heavy users subsidize light users), discourages usage

**Model B: Usage-Based (Proportional)**
- Dividend proportional to subscription value
- Example: Member pays €100/year, gets 2× dividend of €50/year member
- **Pros:** Fair (usage = reward), encourages usage
- **Cons:** Heavy users dominate dividends (but this is how S-Group works)

**Model C: Tenure-Based**
- Dividend weighted by membership length
- Example: 5-year member gets 5× dividend of 1-year member
- **Pros:** Rewards loyalty
- **Cons:** New members get almost nothing (discourages growth)

**Model D: Hybrid (Recommended for Sisu Oy)**
- **Base dividend:** 50% of pool distributed equally to all members
- **Usage bonus:** 50% of pool distributed proportional to subscription value
- **Founding bonus:** Founding members get 2× weight on usage portion
- **Example:**
  - Total dividend pool: €400K
  - Base: €200K / 10,000 members = €20 per member (everyone gets this)
  - Usage: €200K distributed proportional to weighted usage
  - VPN-only member: €20 (base) + €9 (usage) = €29 total
  - Founding member: €20 (base) + €30.50 (usage × 2) = €50.50 total

**Legal Compliance:**
- All models are legal under Osuuskuntalaki
- Bylaws must explicitly state dividend formula
- **S-Group uses Model B** (usage-based: 1-5% cashback based on spending)
- **OP Group uses Model B** (0.4% of banking/insurance/investment usage)

**Recommendation:** Model B (usage-based) with founding member 2× multiplier
- **Rationale:** Aligns with S-Group/OP precedent, fair, encourages usage, rewards commitment

## Part 5: Governance Models

### 5.1 Voting Rights: One Member One Vote vs Weighted

**Legal Framework (Osuuskuntalaki 421/2013):**

**Default:** One member, one vote
- Every member has exactly one vote at General Assembly
- **This is the cooperative principle** (distinguishes from Oy)

**Weighted Voting (Optional):**
- Bylaws can allow multiple votes per member
- **Legal restriction:** One member's votes ≤ 20× another member's votes
- Votes can be weighted by:
  - Number of shares owned
  - Subscription tier
  - Tenure (years as member)
  - Usage (annual subscription value)

**Example:** Sustainable Games Alliance (Finnish osuuskunta)
- Each share = one vote
- But: Member's total votes ≤ 10× any other member's votes
- **This is legal and commonly used**

**Recommendation for Sisu Oy: Modified One Member, One Vote**

**Base:** Everyone gets 1 vote (pure democracy)

**Bonuses (capped at 5× multiplier):**
- Founding member: +1 vote (2 votes total)
- Every year of membership: +0.5 votes (max +2 votes for 4+ years)
- High-tier subscription (€10+/month): +1 vote
- **Maximum votes per member:** 5 votes (1 base + 4 bonus)

**Example voting power:**
- New VPN-only member (€5/month): 1 vote
- 1-year VPN-only member: 1.5 votes
- New founding member (€100/year): 2 votes
- 4-year founding member: 4.5 votes
- 4-year founding member with high-tier (€10/month): 5 votes (capped)

**Rationale:**
- Rewards commitment (founding members, long-term members)
- Prevents newcomers from hijacking governance (4-year member has 5× influence)
- Stays within legal 20× cap (5× ≪ 20×)
- **More democratic than S-Group** (which doesn't weight votes by usage, just dividends)

**Precedent:**
- Many cooperatives use weighted voting (especially agricultural co-ops in EU)
- Finnish law explicitly allows this (with 20× cap)
- **But:** S-Group and OP Group use pure one member, one vote (simplicity > optimization)

**Alternative: Pure One Member, One Vote (S-Group Model)**
- Simplest, most "cooperative"
- Every member equal in governance (no matter when they joined or how much they pay)
- **Downside:** 10,000 new members join on same day, they can outvote 1,000 founding members
- **Mitigation:** Require 1-year membership before voting rights activate (common in cooperatives)

**Sources:**
- [Finnish Cooperative Act voting provisions](https://startcooperative.pellervo.fi/clear-rules-model-rules-for-a-cooperative/)
- [Sustainable Games Alliance statute (Finnish co-op example)](https://sustainablegamesalliance.org/join/statute/)
- [One member, one vote in cooperatives](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ags/jlcoop/46411.html)

### 5.2 Governance Model: Direct vs Representative Democracy

**Two Models:**

**Model A: Direct Democracy (Pure Cooperative)**
- All major decisions require General Assembly vote
- Every member votes on: board elections, budget, new services, partnerships, bylaw changes
- **Pros:** Most democratic, members control everything
- **Cons:** Slow, low participation (most members don't care about every decision)

**Model B: Representative Democracy (S-Group Model)**
- Members elect board of directors (or Members' Council)
- Board makes most decisions (operations, strategy, new services)
- General Assembly votes only on: bylaw changes, dividend distribution, major investments
- **Pros:** Fast, professional management, members can be passive
- **Cons:** Less direct control, risk of board capture

**Recommendation for Sisu Oy: Hybrid Model**

**Board Decides (No Member Vote Required):**
- Operations: Hiring, infrastructure, vendor contracts
- New services: Adding VPN server locations, launching cloud storage
- Pricing: Subscription fee changes (within reason)
- Partnerships: Co-marketing with S-Group, trade unions
- Dividend amount: How much surplus to distribute (but formula fixed by bylaws)

**General Assembly Decides (Member Vote Required):**
- Bylaw changes (requires 2/3 majority)
- Mission changes (requires 75% supermajority)
- Major investments (>€500K)
- Acquisitions or mergers
- Board elections (annual)
- Dividend distribution approval (board proposes, assembly approves)

**Emergency Member Referendum:**
- Any 5% of members can force referendum on any decision
- Example: 500 members (out of 10,000) petition → referendum held within 30 days
- **This is the safety valve** (board can't ignore member will)

**Digital Voting:**
- All votes conducted online (members vote via member portal)
- Proxy voting allowed (delegate your vote to another member)
- **Legal:** Digital voting is legal for Finnish cooperatives (no in-person requirement)

**Board Elections:**
- 7 board seats (staggered 3-year terms)
- Any member can run for board (no qualifications required)
- Elections held annually (2-3 seats per year)
- **Voting:** Modified one member, one vote (as defined above)

**Precedent:**
- S-Group uses Members' Council (elected representatives) + Board (elected by council)
- OP Group uses similar model (cooperative banks elect representatives)
- **Sisu Oy can use simpler model** (direct election of board, no intermediary council)

**Sources:**

- [Finnish cooperative governance](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/principles-of-european-cooperative-law/finland/CE47185F8C23E58F3FB4143BA0016789)

### 5.3 How Often General Assembly? What Requires Member Vote?

**Legal Requirement:**
- General Assembly must be held **at least once per year** (Annual General Meeting)
- Bylaws can require more frequent meetings (quarterly, semi-annual)

**Recommendation for Sisu Oy:**

**Annual General Meeting (AGM) - Mandatory**
- Date: February every year (before tax deadline March 31)
- Agenda:
  1. Board report (year in review)
  2. Financial statements (audited)
  3. Dividend distribution approval
  4. Board elections (2-3 seats)
  5. Any member proposals (submitted 30 days in advance)
- Duration: 2-3 hours (online video call + written comments)

**Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM) - As Needed**
- Called by: Board, or 5% of members petition
- Trigger events: Mission change, major acquisition, bylaw crisis
- Example: "Should we add insurance collective bargaining?" → EGM if board wants member input

**Continuous Voting (Between Meetings)**
- Minor decisions: Board proposes, members vote online (14-day voting window)
- Example: "Should we add Sweden as service region?" → Online vote (no need for full meeting)
- **Quorum:** 10% of members must vote for decision to be valid

**What Requires Member Vote:**

**Always Member Vote:**
- Bylaw amendments (2/3 majority)
- Mission changes (75% supermajority)
- Board elections (simple majority)
- Dividend distribution (simple majority to approve board proposal)
- Major investments >€500K (simple majority)
- Dissolution of cooperative (75% supermajority)

**Board Decides (No Vote):**
- New services (VPN, storage, email)
- Pricing changes (€5/month → €6/month)
- Infrastructure (buy more servers)
- Partnerships (S-Group, OP Group)
- Hiring/firing
- Marketing campaigns

**Gray Area (Board Decides, But Members Can Petition for Vote):**
- Adding new service category (insurance, banking)
- Geographic expansion (launch in Sweden)
- Major strategic pivot
- **If 5% of members petition:** Trigger referendum

## Part 6: Anti-Enshittification Protections

### 6.1 Legal Enforceability of Anti-Enshittification Bylaws

**Question:** Can bylaws legally prevent enshittification?

**Answer:** YES, with caveats.

**What Can Be Legally Guaranteed:**

**1. Anti-Acquisition Protection**
- Bylaw: "Cooperative cannot be acquired or merged without 75% member supermajority vote"
- **Legal:** ✅ Enforceable (bylaws can require supermajority for acquisitions)
- **Effect:** Hostile acquisition impossible (acquirer can't force it)
- **Precedent:** S-Group and OP Group have been independent for 120+ years despite being worth billions

**2. Data Policy Protection**
- Bylaw: "Cooperative shall not sell, share, or use member data for advertising purposes. Any change to data policy requires 75% member supermajority."
- **Legal:** ✅ Enforceable (bylaws can mandate data policies)
- **Effect:** Board cannot unilaterally change data policy (members must approve)
- **Enforcement:** Members can sue cooperative for bylaw violation

**3. Mission Protection**
- Bylaw: "Cooperative's mission is to provide privacy-focused digital services. Any change to mission requires 75% member supermajority."
- **Legal:** ✅ Enforceable (bylaws define purpose)
- **Effect:** Can't pivot to ad-supported model without 75% vote

**4. Open Source Requirement**
- Bylaw: "All cooperative software shall be open source under AGPL-3.0 license. Closing source code requires 75% member supermajority."
- **Legal:** ✅ Enforceable (bylaws can mandate operational practices)
- **Effect:** Can't make code proprietary without member vote

**5. No-Ads Guarantee**
- Bylaw: "Cooperative shall not display advertisements to members. Introduction of ads requires 75% member supermajority."
- **Legal:** ✅ Enforceable (bylaws can prohibit revenue models)
- **Effect:** Can't enshittify by adding ads

**What CANNOT Be Legally Guaranteed:**

**1. Permanent Pricing**
- Bylaw: "VPN shall always cost €5/month" ← NOT enforceable (unrealistic, inflation exists)
- **Alternative:** "Price increases require board approval + member notification 30 days in advance"

**2. Specific Feature Set**
- Bylaw: "VPN must have kill switch, split tunneling, 10+ server locations" ← NOT enforceable (too specific, tech changes)
- **Alternative:** "VPN must meet industry security standards (audited annually)"

**3. Guaranteed Dividends**
- Bylaw: "Members shall receive 10% dividend annually" ← NOT enforceable (can't guarantee profits)
- **Alternative:** "80% of surplus shall be distributed as dividends (if surplus exists)"

### 6.2 Recommended Anti-Enshittification Bylaws for Sisu Oy

**Article X: Anti-Enshittification Protections**

**Section X.1: Anti-Acquisition**
> "The Cooperative may not be acquired, merged, or sold to any third party without the approval of at least 75% of voting members at a General Assembly. Any acquisition proposal must be communicated to all members at least 60 days in advance, with full disclosure of terms, acquirer identity, and impact on member services."

**Section X.2: Data Sovereignty**
> "The Cooperative shall:
> (a) Store all member data within Finland or EEA, using Finnish/EU data centers only.
> (b) Never sell, share, or license member data to third parties for advertising, training AI models, or any purpose beyond service delivery.
> (c) Require 75% member supermajority to change this data policy."

**Section X.3: No-Ads Guarantee**
> "The Cooperative's services shall be ad-free. Introduction of advertising, sponsored content, or any third-party monetization of member attention requires 75% member supermajority."

**Section X.4: Open Source Commitment**
> "All Cooperative software (VPN clients, servers, web services) shall be published as open source under AGPL-3.0 license or equivalent copyleft license. Closing source code or changing to permissive license requires 75% member supermajority."

**Section X.5: Mission Lock**
> "The Cooperative's mission is to provide privacy-focused, member-owned digital services that resist enshittification. Any change to this mission, including pivot to different business model, requires 75% member supermajority."

**Section X.6: Dividend Distribution Formula**
> "At least 80% of annual surplus shall be distributed to members as dividends (ylijäämänpalautus), proportional to usage. Maximum 20% may be retained for reserves. Changing this 80/20 split requires 75% member supermajority."

**Section X.7: Security Audit Requirement**
> "The Cooperative shall conduct independent security audits at least annually, publishing full results to members. Skipping an audit or withholding results requires 75% member supermajority (with justification)."

**Section X.8: Member Referendum Right**
> "Any 5% of members may petition for referendum on any decision. Upon valid petition, referendum must be held within 30 days, with result binding on the Board."

**Legal Effect:**
- These bylaws are **legally binding** on the Board and cooperative
- Members can sue for breach of bylaws
- Board members who violate bylaws can be removed by member vote
- **Enshittification becomes illegal** (bylaw violation)

**Enforcement Mechanism:**
- Members can call Extraordinary General Meeting if bylaws violated
- Members can sue cooperative in Finnish courts
- Members can vote out board if they approve bylaw violation
- **Practical effect:** Board knows any enshittification attempt will fail (members will vote them out)

## Part 7: Banking License Compatibility

### 7.1 Can Osuuskunta Get Banking License?

**Answer:** YES. OP Group proves this.

**OP Group Structure:**
- 102 independent cooperative banks (each is an osuuskunta)
- OP Cooperative (central organization, also osuuskunta)
- All supervised by European Central Bank (ECB)
- Combined assets: €149 billion (2023)

**How OP Group Does It:**
1. Each local cooperative bank gets banking license from Finnish Financial Supervisory Authority (FIN-FSA)
2. Joint and several liability: All 102 banks insure each other (mutual support)
3. Central cooperative (OP Cooperative) provides shared services (IT, compliance, risk management)
4. **Key:** Member ownership doesn't conflict with banking regulations (just need strong governance)

**Requirements for Cooperative Banking License (Same as Oy):**
- Minimum capital: €5M (for basic banking), €18M (for investment services)
- Professional management (board + CEO with banking experience)
- Comprehensive risk management
- ECB supervision (if "significant institution")

**Sisu Oy Implications:**
- **Phase 1-3:** No banking license needed (VPN, storage, energy bargaining are not banking)
- **Phase 4 (if ever):** Could add banking services (accounts, payments, credit cards)
  - Would need banking license
  - Cooperative structure NOT a barrier (OP proves it works)
  - **But:** Requires €5M+ capital, years of regulatory process
- **Conclusion:** Osuuskunta is future-proof for banking expansion (if desired)

## Part 8: Adding New Services - Flexibility

### 8.1 Does Adding New Service Require Member Vote?

**Legal Answer:** Depends on bylaws.

**Default (No Bylaw Restriction):**
- Board can add new services without member vote
- Rationale: Board is elected to make operational decisions
- Adding VPN → storage → email is "operations" (not fundamental change)

**Recommended for Sisu Oy: Board Decides, But Members Can Object**

**Bylaw:**
> "The Board may add new services without member vote, provided:
> (a) New service aligns with Cooperative's mission (privacy-focused digital services)
> (b) Board notifies members 30 days in advance
> (c) If 5% of members petition, service addition goes to referendum"

**Example:**
- Board decides: "We're adding cloud storage (200GB for €10/month)"
- Notification sent to all members 30 days in advance
- If 500 members (5% of 10,000) petition: "We don't want storage, focus on VPN!" → Referendum held
- If <500 petition: Storage launches as planned

**Major Service Categories (Require Member Vote):**
- **Banking services:** Accounts, payments, loans (requires member approval + banking license)
- **Insurance services:** Collective insurance underwriting (requires member approval + insurance license)
- **Outside cooperative mission:** If Sisu Oy wanted to launch "Sisu Ride-Sharing" → member vote required

**Flexibility:**
- Osuuskunta is very flexible (bylaws can be amended with 2/3 vote)
- Board can pivot quickly (no shareholder approval needed for operations)
- **More flexible than Oy** (where major changes may trigger securities regulations)

## Part 9: Liability and Risk

### 9.1 Member Liability for Cooperative Debts

**Legal Answer:** NO. Members have limited liability.

**Per Osuuskuntalaki:**
> "A cooperative is a legal entity separate from its members. Members and other owners of shares do not have personal liability for the cooperative's obligations."

**What This Means:**
- Cooperative goes bankrupt → members lose membership fee + any share capital paid
- **But:** Members NOT liable for cooperative's debts beyond that
- Creditors cannot sue members personally
- **Identical to Oy limited liability**

**Example:**
- Sisu Oy collapses with €1M debt to Hetzner (server hosting)
- Members paid €100 founding membership
- **Members lose:** €100 (membership fee)
- **Members do NOT owe:** Any portion of €1M debt
- Hetzner can only claim against cooperative's assets (not members' personal assets)

**Insurance Requirements:**
- **No mandatory insurance** for basic cooperative (unlike some professions)
- **Recommended:** Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance (protects board members from personal liability)
- **If banking:** Deposit insurance required (€100K per customer, provided by EU/Finnish system)
- **If insurance services:** Professional indemnity insurance required

**Sources:**
- [Finnish Cooperative Act 421/2013](https://www.finlex.fi/en/legislation/2013/421)
