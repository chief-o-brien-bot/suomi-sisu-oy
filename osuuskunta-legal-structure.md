## 1. Legal Structure Basics

### What is an Osuuskunta?

An osuuskunta (cooperative) is a business structure where members collectively own the organization and exercise decision-making power democratically. Regulated by [Finnish Cooperatives Act (Osuuskuntalaki 421/2013)](https://www.finlex.fi/en/legislation/2013/421).

**Formation Requirements:**
- No minimum members (typically 3+ recommended)
- No minimum capital requirement
- Register with PRH (Finnish Patent and Registration Office) within 3 months
- Registration fee: €175
- At least one board member + deputy must be EEA-domiciled

**Sources:**
- [Finnish Cooperatives Act - Finlex](https://www.finlex.fi/en/legislation/2013/421)
- [Starting a Cooperative in Finland - Pellervo](https://startcooperative.pellervo.fi/clear-rules-model-rules-for-a-cooperative/)
- [Setting up a cooperative - Suomi.fi](https://www.suomi.fi/company/starting-a-business/forms-of-enterprise/guide/cooperative/setting-up-cooperative)

### Osuuskunta vs Oy (Limited Company)

| Aspect | Osuuskunta | Oy |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Voting | One member, one vote | Votes proportional to shares |
| Acquisition vulnerability | Very low (can't buy voting control) | High (51% shares = control) |
| Purpose | Support members' economy | Maximize shareholder profit |
| Profit distribution | Ylijäämänpalautus (usage-based) | Dividends (share-based) |

**Key advantage for Sisu Oy:** [One member, one vote structure](https://ncbaclusa.coop/blog/differences-between-cooperatives-and-corporations/) inherently prevents hostile takeover, even if someone acquires many memberships.

**Sources:**
- [Types of Companies in Finland](https://companyformationfinland.com/types-of-companies-in-finland/)

## 2. Membership Structure

### Automatic vs Application-Based Membership

Finnish cooperative law allows flexibility. [According to Pellervo guidance](https://startcooperative.pellervo.fi/clear-rules-model-rules-for-a-cooperative/compulsory-stipulations-to-be-included-in-the-rules/), membership typically requires application + board approval. However, bylaws can streamline this to make membership automatic upon payment.

**Sisu Oy Recommendation: Hybrid Three-Tier Model**

1. **Free trials:** Not members (trial only)
2. **VPN-Only (€5/month):** Customers, not members initially
3. **Founding Members (€100/year):** Automatic membership + enhanced benefits
4. **Conversion path:** VPN-only users offered membership after 12 months (€50 fee)

**Rationale:** Balances trust-building (VPN-only test service) with cooperative values (long-term users become owners).

**Precedents:**
- [S-Group](https://www.s-kanava.fi/en/s-groups-cooperatives/): €5 membership fee, ~78% of Finnish households are members
- [OP Group](https://www.op.fi/en/private-customers/benefits/op-cooperative-bank-owner-customer/together): €100 share purchase for membership

## 3. Dividend Distribution (Ylijäämänpalautus)

### Finnish Cooperative Dividend System

**Ylijäämänpalautus** = "surplus return" distributed proportionally to **usage**, not ownership stake.

[According to S-Group research](https://www.s-kanava.fi/bonus/yjp/):
> "Ylijäämänpalautus is one of the cooperative principles according to which surplus remaining after investments can be distributed back to cooperative members in proportion to their use of services."

**S-Group Model:**
- Members earn "Bonus" on purchases (up to 5% cashback)
- Additional ylijäämänpalautus paid annually if results allow
- 2.5 million members, average €329M in dividends (based on 2018 data)

**OP Group Model:**
- [Owner-customers earn OP bonuses](https://www.op.fi/private-customers/owner-customer-and-benefits/op-bonuses) through banking/insurance transactions
- "When OP Financial Group does well, owner-customers benefit"

**Sisu Oy Recommendation:** Usage-based with Founding Member Bonus

**Formula:**
1. Usage units: VPN-only = 60 units/year, Founding Member = 200 units/year (2× multiplier)
2. Surplus pool divided by total usage units
3. Founding member bonus expires after 5 years

**Example:** €100K surplus / 1.5M total units = €0.067 per unit
- VPN-only: 60 units × €0.067 = €4 dividend
- Founding member: 200 units × €0.067 = €13.40 dividend

**Sources:**
- [S-Group ylijäämänpalautus](https://kymenseudunosuuskauppa.fi/tietoa-meista/kso-ylijaamanpalautus/)
- [The Cooperative That Could - The American Prospect](https://prospect.org/world/2023-10-11-cooperative-that-could-s-group-finland/)

## 4. Governance Structure

### Finnish Cooperative Governance Requirements

**Mandatory bodies:**
1. General Meeting (supreme authority)
2. Board of Directors (minimum 1 member, typically 3-7)
3. Managing Director (optional for small cooperatives)

**Voting:** [One member, one vote](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/principles-of-european-cooperative-law/finland/CE47185F8C23E58F3FB4143BA0016789) (default, can be modified in bylaws)

**Digital voting:** [Not explicitly regulated](https://www.polyas.com/cooperatives/board-elections/online). Bylaws can authorize it. Recommended for Sisu Oy (tech-native, geographically distributed members).

**Sisu Oy Recommendation: Phased Governance**

**Year 1-2:** Founding team governance (3 founders, no elections yet)
**Year 2-3:** Advisory Council (9 elected members, advise founders)
**Year 3+:** Full democratic governance (elected board, member votes on major decisions)

**Rationale:** Matches governance complexity to cooperative scale. Startups need speed; mature cooperatives need democracy.

**Sources:**
- [Cooperative governance in Finland - Suomi.fi](https://www.suomi.fi/yritykselle/yrityksen-perustaminen/yritysmuodot/opas/osuuskunta/osuuskunnan-hallinto-ja-toimielimet)
- [Digital voting for cooperatives - POLYAS](https://www.polyas.com/cooperatives/board-elections/online)

## 5. Anti-Enshittification Legal Protections

### What's Enforceable in Bylaws?

**Protection 1: Hostile Takeover Prevention ✅ ENFORCEABLE**

[One member, one vote](https://ncbaclusa.coop/blog/differences-between-cooperatives-and-corporations/) inherently prevents acquisition:
- Acquiring memberships ≠ proportional voting power
- To acquire cooperative, buyer must convince 75% of members to vote for it

**Bylaw clause:**
> "Any proposed merger, acquisition, or sale requires 75% supermajority vote with 30% quorum."

**Real-world proof:** [S-Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S_Group) has prevented acquisition for 120+ years using this model.

**Protection 2: Data Privacy ✅ ENFORCEABLE (with limits)**

**Bylaw clause:**
> "The cooperative shall not sell member data. This provision requires 90% supermajority to amend."

**Enforceable:** Yes. Members can sue if violated. However, future members could vote to change (democracy).

**Protection 3: Ad-Free Guarantee ✅ ENFORCEABLE (with limits)**

**Bylaw clause:**
> "No third-party advertising. Revenue from member fees only. Requires 80% supermajority to amend."

**Protection 4: Mission Lock ✅ ENFORCEABLE**

**Bylaw clause:**
> "Mission: provide member-owned digital services and collective bargaining. Change requires 80% supermajority + 40% quorum."

### Comparison: Cooperative vs Oy Enshittification Risk

| Risk | Oy | Osuuskunta |
|------|-----|------------|
| Hostile takeover | High (51% shares) | Very Low (one vote per member) |
| Sell to corporation | Easy (board decides) | Hard (member supermajority required) |
| Pivot to ads | Easy (board decides) | Hard (bylaw amendment required) |

- [The Cooperative Hostile Takeover - GEO Coop](https://geo.coop/story/cooperative-hostile-takeover)

## 7. Adding New Services (Operational Flexibility)

**Does adding new services require member vote? Depends on bylaws.**

**Sisu Oy Recommendation:** Broad scope in bylaws allows board flexibility.

**Bylaw clause:**
> "Purpose: provide digital services and collective bargaining for commodities, including but not limited to: internet privacy, cloud storage, energy, insurance, telecom, and related technologies."

**Board can decide (no vote):** Launching services within scope
**Member vote required:** Changing scope, major investments (>€1M), mergers

**Rationale:** Maintains operational speed while preserving democratic control over strategic changes.

**Sources:**

## 8. Formation Roadmap

**Month 1: Preparation**
- Hire Finnish cooperative lawyer (€5-10K)
- Draft bylaws
- Prepare constitution agreement
- Founding members sign (minimum 3)

**Month 2: Registration**
- File with PRH (€175)
- Wait 3-5 weeks for approval
- Receive business ID (Y-tunnus)
- Open business bank account

**Month 3: Post-Registration**
- Business accounts, accounting software
- Insurance (cyber liability, general liability, D&O)
- VAT registration, GDPR compliance

**Total timeline:** ~2 months  
**Total costs:** €15-25K (lawyer €5-10K, registration €175, insurance €5-10K, accounting €2-5K)

**Recommended lawyers:** Contact [Pellervo](https://startcooperative.pellervo.fi/) for cooperative law specialist referrals.

## 9. Liability and Risk

### Member Liability

[Members have limited liability](https://companyformationfinland.com/types-of-companies-in-finland/): NOT personally liable for cooperative's debts beyond capital contribution (membership fee/shares).

**Example:** Member pays €100 founding fee. Cooperative goes bankrupt with €1M debts. Member loses €100 (already paid), but personal assets protected.

**Insurance Requirements:**
- General liability: €1-2M coverage
- Cyber liability: €5-10M coverage (critical for VPN/storage)
- Directors & Officers insurance
- **Estimated cost:** €10-20K/year

## 11. Osuuskunta vs Oy: Final Verdict for Sisu Oy

**Winner: Osuuskunta**

**Why:**
1. **Anti-enshittification:** Structural (one member one vote) vs weak (acquirable)
2. **Cultural fit:** 78% of Finns are S-Group members (understand cooperative model)
3. **Member alignment:** Built-in member-benefit mandate
4. **Hostile takeover risk:** Near-zero vs high

**Only choose Oy if:** Planning to raise VC funding (VCs want equity + board control)
